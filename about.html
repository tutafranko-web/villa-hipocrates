Vrboska, Hvar Island

A Place of Quiet Grandeur

Villa Harpocrates stands quietly at the edge of Vrboska — one of the least-visited and most beautiful villages on Hvar — where the stone walls of old houses meet the shimmer of a sheltered harbour. From here, the island opens in every direction: coves that require no reservations, pine forests that ask nothing but your footsteps, and a sea so blue it seems manufactured for postcards.

The villa is a complex of buildings set within a private garden — a central house surrounded by three guest structures, each designed with the same commitment to quiet luxury. Stone floors. High-thread-count linen. Silence at midnight. The property has welcomed guests for more than a decade, accumulating a legacy of five-star reviews and returning visitors who will rent nowhere else on the island.

Nine hundred metres separates the front gate from Oskorušica cove. You will cover the distance on foot or by bicycle, arriving at water that few tourists ever find. This is the rhythm of Villa Harpocrates — effortless, private, entirely your own.
