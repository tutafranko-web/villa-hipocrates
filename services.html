The Island

Explore Hvar

Hidden Coves & Beaches

Oskorušica cove is a 900m walk. Maslinica, Žukova, Zavala, and Mudri Dolac are reachable by bike. No crowds, no music — just clear water and stone.

Restaurants & Konobas

Vrboska village has exceptional local seafood restaurants just 2km away. Stari Grad's Old Town has candlelit terraces for romantic dinners overlooking the harbour.

Stari Grad Old Town

A UNESCO World Heritage Site 4km away. One of the oldest towns in Europe, with 2,400 years of continuous settlement. Walk the Chora agricultural plain at sunset.

Lavender Fields

Hvar is the lavender island of Croatia. Ride the mountain bikes through fields of purple in June and July — one of the most extraordinary landscapes in the Mediterranean.

Hvar Town

20km west: the social heartbeat of the island. St. Stephen's Square, the Renaissance fortress, beach clubs, and a superyacht harbour that draws the world's elite.

Pakleni Islands

Accessible by water taxi from Hvar Town, these pine-covered islets have clear blue lagoons, naturist beaches, and small restaurants — a day trip not to miss.
